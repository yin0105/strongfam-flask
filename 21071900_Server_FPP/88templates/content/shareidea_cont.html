AT THE SECONDARY LEVEL -- BROADLY DEFINED

Some thoughts before sharing your idea

  • Most of this website is about grants for researchers. Taking time to become familiar with it may pay dividends when you share your idea.
  • If you are excited about your idea likely we will be too.
  • Another reason to apply for funding might be that despite numerous states having curriculum standards on the subject, little scholarly work has been found on teaching parenting at the secondary level. This may indicate significant room for researching the area.
  • Currently, the input form must be completed in a single session. There is no ability to save and return later to the work. We suggest easiest may be to review the imput pages, prepare your information separately, and simply copy and paste it into the input form.
  • Changes may be made to any of the data until the SUBMIT button is pressed. Once pressed, the system will respond with your Project ID Number and Submission Date confirming the data has been accepted by the system.
  • After acceptance the data may not be changed although a revised submission may be input to replace a previous one.
  • Please consider that although this form may be complete, the application is not until copies of your budget and organization’s IRS determination letter have also been entered.

How to share your idea

Entering your idea. Ideas are shared by input here.

Can I revise my idea? Yes. Your Idea can be revised anytime before the submission is complete by clicking the "Revise a Shared Idea" button below. This allows entering a new idea which replaces the old one and can be done multiple times. However, once the My Idea input form has been completed and all documents are submitted, your idea is locked for review and no futhre changes can be made.

What information is requested?

  • The organization’s full name, website address and physical address.
  • The Principal Investigator’s preferred title, name, email address, and telephone number.
  • Optional: Another contact’s person’s preferred title, name, email address, and telephone number.
  • The total grant amount requested (may be $55,000 or less and not more than the project budget total).
  • The project name.
  • Research goal (360 characters (about 60 words) max).
  • Describe the project including research methods, data to be collected and outcomes anticipated (2400 characters (about 400 words) max).
  • Tell us about the PI and others involved including publishing history if not on a CV for which an email address has been entered on the submission form. (1200 characters (about 200 words) max).
  • How is this project relevant to understanding the efficacy of teaching parenting and child development in secondary schools? (2400 characters (about 400 words) max).
  • How will you disseminate the results of your research? (900 characters (about 150 words) max).
  • Is there anything else you would like us to know? (optional - 900 characters (about 150 words) max).

What documents are requested?

  • The organization’s IRS determination letter classifying it as a Public Charity
  • A completed project Budget Worksheet downloadable here.
